Time Expressions in Past

When talking about the past, we often use specific time expressions to indicate when something happened. These expressions help clarify the timing and duration of past actions. Common examples include:

  • in: Used with years, months, and periods of time. Example: I was born in 1990.
  • at: Used with specific times. Example: We met at 3 o'clock.
  • on: Used with days and dates. Example: I went to the party on Saturday.
  • when: Used to link an event with a specific point in time. Example: I started school when I was five years old.
  • for: Used to describe the duration of an action. Example: We lived there for three years.
