Present Simple 'Be' Positive

Superlatives are used to describe the highest degree of a quality among three or more things. They often end in "-est" (e.g., tallest, smartest) or are formed with "most" (e.g., most beautiful, most interesting).

Use

Superlatives help us express the extreme qualities of someone or something in comparison to a larger group.

Example:

John is the smartest student in the class.

Superlatives

Practicing Superlatives in English

1) John is the

(smart) student in the class.

[ smartest ]

2) This is the

(beautiful) place I’ve ever visited.

[ most beautiful ]

3) That was the

(exciting) game of the season.

[ most exciting ]

4) She is the

(kind) person I know.

[ kindest ]

5) Of all the mountains, Mount Everest is the

(high).

[ highest ]

6) This is the

(bad) headache I’ve ever had.

[ worst ]

7) That was the

(funny) joke I’ve heard today.

[ funniest ]

8) The Sahara is the

(large) desert in the world.

[ largest ]

9) Of all my friends, Sarah is the

(good) at math.

[ best ]

10) That is the

(expensive) car in the showroom.

[ most expensive ]



Listening Exercise

Conversation 1

Woman: What is the fastest way to get to the airport?

Man: By taxi, but it’s the most expensive.

Woman: What’s the cheapest?

Man: The local bus. But, it's by far the slowest way to get there.

Woman: Hmm, I'd better spring for a taxi then.

Man: Yeah, it’s best if you do.

Conversation 2

Woman: What is the best place to visit in your country?

Man: I think the west coast. It has the best weather.

Woman: What about the cost?

Man: The costs are very high. Our most expensive cities are there.

Woman: Oh, that’s too bad. What about the food?

Man: It’s great. It has the best restaurants in the country.

Conversation 3

Woman: How was the movie last night?

Man: Oh, it was great. It was the scariest movie I’ve ever seen.

Woman: Oh, really? That scary?

Man: Yes, I was screaming throughout the film.

Woman: Was it crowded?

Man: Yeah, it was the most packed I’ve ever seen the theater.

Conversation 4

Woman: What is the oldest city in your country?

Man: I think it is the capital.

Woman: Is it the biggest?

Man: It was, but not anymore.

Woman: Why are people moving out?

Man: Well, it’s not the most modern place to live.
